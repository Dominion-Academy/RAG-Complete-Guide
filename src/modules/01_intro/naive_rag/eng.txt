1. Politics / Crisis
Dry Hollow refuses to join the Raft until the crack season ends.
The Council of Elders of the three Southern Crags declared a "memorial blockade": their individuals will not fuse into Living Ships until the North returns the stolen fragments of the Well. 12,000 eggs in the tidal zones are at stake.

2. Accidents
Young Qi-Lir group dies after creating a "dry mirror" in an abandoned geodesic mine.
Wanting to test their mental limits, the teenagers polished the walls with basalt dust to a mirror shine. All seven fell into the catatonia of "the Void Within". Rescuers cannot approach due to the risk of secondary reflection.

3. Culture
Premiere of the opera "The Tear That Never Burst" fails — singers forget the lyrics.
The performers used old cracks on their backs, but during the "Waiting at Noon" aria, the ceramic carriers of two soloists crumbled from emotional overload. The audience demanded their moisture back as refunds.

4. Science
Physicists of the Whispering Crags disprove the "Eternal Turnover" theory.
New calculations show that Xanth's axis does not tumble randomly but adjusts to the breathing of the star Atropos. A "gravitational echo" phenomenon is discovered — the planet remembers every past fall.

5. Crime
Smuggler caught stealing water from sacred thermoses at cemeteries.
The perpetrator resold H₂O to Black Collectors, who extract "dry tears" — a drug that creates a false sensation of a full Well. Punishment: lifelong fusion with a cargo Raft without voting rights.

6. Sports
Individual from the Shadow Hollow wins the acoustic knife fighting championship.
The final lasted 4 hours — opponents used ultrasound to crack each other's limbs. The winner lost two arms but received the main prize: the right to carve a new memory-crack onto the Supreme Elder's back.

7. Weather (Astronomy)
Atropos emits an unexpected flare — all echolocation tendrils temporarily blinded.
The burst of hard radiation will last 2 days. Qi-Lir are forced to move by touch. 400 collisions recorded at intersections. Medics advise not to panic and not to open "third eyes" (chest serpentine arms) unless necessary.

8. Economy
Prices of crushed ceramic hit an all-time low.
The market is oversaturated after the collapse of the Western Escarpment. Even the poorest now can repair small cracks. Dentists are losing money: cleaning of resonator pits is 70% cheaper.

9. Religion
Cult of the Waiting Tear splits into two sects.
"Wet" ones demand accelerating the gathering of the entire ocean into one Tear at any cost. "Dry" ones propose to preserve water individually within each body. Thermoacoustic forks come into play. The first temple (Dry Hollow) is flooded with molten silicone.

10. Technology
First "Solo Raft" unveiled — a ship made from a single body.
A mad engineer fused himself, growing 18 additional serpentine arms and coating himself with six-layer armor. He claims he no longer needs a kin. The community calls him "The Abomination Reef". So far, he does not sink.

11. Medicine
Epidemic of "false cracks" recorded — a disease of fake memories.
Carriers start boasting of feats they never performed. Cracks on their backs grow spontaneously but contain empty data. Treatment: complete erasure of the Well, followed by repopulation from three donors.

12. Transport
Intercity Raft of Memory crashes: 200 bodies decouple during a storm.
Cause: low-quality cement secreted by overworked elders. Survivors complain they now "hear other people's deaths" in their cracks. Investigation led by the Council of Wet Traces.

13. Education
Schools cancel "Mirror Self-Defense" lessons.
Teachers noticed that the practice of looking into reflections (to build immunity) causes irreversible anxiety in 30% of students. Instead, a "Hum of the Void" course is introduced — outdoor meditation without echo.

14. Society & Celebrity
Oldest Qi-Lir (480 cycles) celebrates anniversary with a new crack.
The birthday individual, Shshsh-Krak (translation: "Bone-That-Remembers-Everything"), voluntarily accepted onto their back a recording of how, in their youth, they dropped an egg into a fissure. Family is delighted. Guests were served burnt sand and distilled saliva.

15. Environment
Oceans begin "whispering" 3 months before the Great Turnover.
Hydrophones record low-frequency vibrations that Qi-Lir interpret as the voices of drowned ancestors. Scientists debate: physics or mass hallucination? Fishermen refuse to go to sea.

16. Military
Border crag units placed on "dry mode".
Soldiers are forbidden to drink water for 10 days before duty. This increases ceramic brittleness but makes their ultrasonic scream lethal at 50 meters. Commanders say: "Better we crack than the enemy."

17. Architecture
First building constructed without a single right angle — to avoid reflective surfaces.
The new terminal of the Hollow of Dead Mirrors is shaped like a flattened bubble. Inside, everything is covered with soft moss that absorbs all signals. Residents complain of claustrophobia but admit — "the Void" has not appeared once.

18. Crime
Black market for "living bones" flourishes after the Escarpment collapse.
Gangs dig up fresh corpses and cut out hollow bones filled with gas. These are made into illegal flying devices — "Whisperers". Police cannot keep up: the bones are too light and quickly fly off into the stratosphere.

19. Art
Artist creates a portrait titled "The True Void" — a completely smooth ceramic slab.
At the opening, 90% of viewers fall into catatonia. The remaining 10% smash the sculpture with thermoacoustic forks. The author is arrested under the article "Mass induction of mirror horror". The artist insists it is "the most honest work of the cycle".

20. Forecast for the next cycle
The Great Turnover is expected 8 days early.
Orbital stations have detected an acceleration of the core's rotation. All Qi-Lir must urgently complete fusion into Rafts and leave the lowlands. Oceans will rise by 400 meters. Stock up on cement and do not look at the water — it reflects. End of bulletin.
